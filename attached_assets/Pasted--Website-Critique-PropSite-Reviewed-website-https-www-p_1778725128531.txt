## Main Issues and Risks

The most important weakness is that the site does not yet fully address **trust and operational risk**. A real estate agent will naturally ask: How does PropSite get MLS access? Does this require MLS board approval? Is it IDX-compliant? What data is stored? Who owns the domain? Who owns the leads? What happens when a listing is withdrawn, expires, or closes? Can the agent edit copy or photos if the MLS data is wrong? These questions are not minor details; they are likely conversion blockers for a product built around automated MLS-triggered publishing.

The second issue is that the live examples and testimonials are useful but could carry more credibility. The testimonials are attributed to first names and last initials, such as Sarah R., David M., and Tracy L., with brokerage references.[1] That is better than anonymous praise, but it still feels somewhat lightweight for a product asking agents to trust automation with their listings. Where permitted, the site would benefit from agent headshots, full names, brokerage logos, linked case studies, or a short note explaining that these are beta users, customers, or representative examples.
